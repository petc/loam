She does something she shouldn't.

She has been sitting with the expedited timeline since Wednesday, turning it over, and on Friday morning she goes to the archive before her queue opens and pulls the original ZVA request form.

It takes her three attempts to find the right access path. She is not blocked from it — she has system-level access to historical items in her processing category — but the path is buried, the way things are buried when they are not intended to be found by people at her level.

The request form.

Initiation: January 14, 2047. Requested by: Division 4, Strategic Infrastructure. Authorising officer: [field redacted].

She has not seen a redacted authorising officer before.

She keeps reading.

*Rationale for assessment: site presents strategic infrastructure corridor potential consistent with Northern Expansion planning framework. Assessment to evaluate feasibility of recategorization and timeline for remediation.*

Northern Expansion.

She searches the term. The Northern Expansion planning framework is a public document — she finds it easily. A ten-year infrastructure plan for extending the city's logistics and transit network into currently underutilised zones. Approved by the Accord's planning authority in 2044.

She looks up Sector 9 on the Northern Expansion map.

The decommissioned rail yard is on the map. It is labelled: *Priority Corridor — Phase 2.*

Phase 2 begins in 2048.

She sits back.

The zone assessment was initiated not because of environmental anomalies or demographic concern or even the relevance score. It was initiated because someone in Division 4 looked at the Northern Expansion map and decided that Phase 2 couldn't wait.

The ZVA is not diagnostic.

It is a justification.

It was always going to reach the same conclusion.

She closes the files.

She thinks about Elias's hands, dark with soil in the creases. She thinks about the south bed and the five small seedlings and the rose moving along the gantry in the direction it chose. She thinks about what it means to know what a place is and to participate in the process of deciding it is worth less than a logistics corridor.

She thinks: I have been participating in this since I began.

She thinks: I don't know how to stop.

She opens her morning queue.

Fifty-three items.

She begins.
