She cannot close the item.

The ZVA status update — secondary review complete, outcome as expected, recommendation to proceed to remediation order — appeared in her monitoring queue on Tuesday. It is not hers to close. Division 4 will close it. But she can see it, and she has been seeing it for three days, and it sits in her field of vision the way a sound sits in a room after the source of it has stopped.

Her supervisor asks on Wednesday.

"You look off," he says. Not unkindly. He is not an unkind man; she has never found him unkind. He operates within his constraints the way most people operate within their constraints: efficiently, without examining the walls.

"I'm fine," she says.

"You've been reviewing Sector 9 for weeks." He looks at his tablet. "That initial flag was resolved in March."

"Follow-up," she says. "The macro-flag. I've been monitoring."

He looks at her.

"That's Strategic's scope," he says.

"I know." She meets his eyes. "I wanted to understand the full picture."

He is quiet for a moment.

"Mara," he says. "There are pictures that aren't ours to understand."

He says it gently. He does not raise his voice. He says it the way you say something to someone you would prefer not to say it to, but which needs to be said.

She nods.

He goes back to his desk.

She turns back to her terminal. Opens her queue. Fifty-one items, most of them nothing. Sensor drift, close. Census lag, flag for quarterly, close. Infrastructure anomaly, maintenance scheduled, close.

She works through twenty-three items.

She stops.

She looks at the window. The city, even and constant, doing what cities do.

She thinks about Elias saying: *I made a formal objection in a committee meeting and it was documented as a technical concern, noted.* She thinks about the shape of what's available to her — forms, flags, queues, items to be processed and closed — and the gap between the shape of her tools and the shape of the problem.

She does not open a document.

She takes the notebook from her bag instead — plain, bought at the Wednesday market months ago for no reason she could name at the time — and turns to a blank page. Paper. Nothing the system can index, archive, or pull from a drive she does not control.

She starts writing.

She doesn't know yet what it is.

She keeps writing.
