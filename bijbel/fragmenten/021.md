She goes back to the database on Wednesday morning.

She is not sure what she is looking for. She looks anyway — because she is, fundamentally, a person who processes queues, and the thing she has been carrying since Sunday is unprocessed, and she cannot close it without understanding it.

She pulls the ZVA item. Status: Preliminary analysis complete. Secondary review pending.

She looks at the initiation date again. January 14th. Preliminary analysis complete as of March 3rd. Seven weeks of analysis.

She runs a comparison. How long does preliminary analysis typically take on a Zone Viability Assessment?

She pulls three completed ZVAs from the archive — zones that went through the full process. The preliminary analysis durations: eleven days, nine days, fourteen days.

Seven weeks.

She reads the preliminary report summary again. It is more restricted than the base item — she can see the conclusions, not the methodology.

:::accord
ACCORD ZONE VIABILITY ASSESSMENT — SECTOR 9
Secondary review status: Complete
Outcome: Below viability threshold (all five primary indicators)
Recommendation: Proceed to remediation order
Timeline: Expedited
:::

Expedited.

She sits with this.

Standard secondary review: ninety days. Expedited: thirty.

She checks the initiation date on the secondary review. It was initiated on March 3rd, the same day the preliminary analysis completed.

She counts forward thirty days.

April 2nd.

Today.

She looks at the database. The status has just updated. She watches the timestamp refresh.

She does not close the browser. She sits with it open and thinks about the gap between seven weeks of analysis and the standard nine-to-fourteen days. She thinks about what you do for seven weeks when you are assessing a zone for viability.

You are not assessing the zone.

You are building the case.

She closes the database.

She opens a new search.

She is looking for something she does not have a name for yet: a gap, a leverage point, the difference between what the record shows and what is actually there.

She is very good at finding differences.
