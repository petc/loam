Not a single event. A cascade.

Between 2031 and 2039, overlapping crises — economic, climatic, political — compounded faster than societies could adapt. Supply chains shortened. Populations consolidated. Institutions that had seemed permanent quietly stopped functioning.

Nobody declared it. There was no moment when the world ended. Things simply became, over years, less possible than they had been. Then less possible again.

The generation born into it — Mara's generation — does not experience it as loss. They have no before to compare it to. This is simply the shape of the world.

Elias's generation remembers. Most of them have stopped talking about it.
