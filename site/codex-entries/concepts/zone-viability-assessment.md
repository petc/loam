The Accord's own definition, published and available to any citizen who looks: a technical process to evaluate the ongoing resource efficiency of infrastructure-designated zones, considering usage metrics, population density, economic productivity, infrastructure condition, and projected maintenance cost over a standard planning cycle.

In practice: the procedure that decides whether a place still earns its upkeep. A ZVA that clears preliminary analysis moves to secondary review. A zone that fails secondary review moves toward remediation — the administrative word for what happens to the people who live there.

Most ZVAs, Accord staff will tell you, come to nothing. Most are routine, low-priority, filed and forgotten.

Not all of them.
