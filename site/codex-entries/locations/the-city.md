Unnamed, deliberately. It could be any city that ran the same numbers and arrived at the same answer.

It is organised into Sectors, numbered rather than named. The numbering is not officially a judgement. Everyone treats it as one anyway. The centre is dense, expensive, fully covered by The Accord. The edge is cheaper, grittier, and worth less to optimise.

Not much grows here that wasn't approved first. What green space exists is maintained by The Accord's grounds services — efficient species, nothing that goes its own way.

Systems make most of the sound: ventilation, logistics traffic, the low hum of data infrastructure in newer walls. People move with purpose. Standing still without a reason draws attention — not from anyone in particular. From what is watching.
