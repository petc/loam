The city's edge. Older buildings, some from before The Contraction — cracked concrete, repairs patched over repairs. Sensor coverage here is thin; The Accord monitors through basic infrastructure, not the fine mesh that covers the centre.

People end up here because they cannot leave, or will not. More elderly residents than the average. More people with low relevance scores who keep going anyway.

There is a market on Wednesdays. Not permitted. Not banned either. The Accord ignores it — the footprint is too small to be worth optimising.

Elias Voss rents a room here from a man who is no longer alive. The system has not noticed. Nobody has checked.
